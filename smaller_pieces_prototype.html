It's 4:17 AM and I'm staring at the ceiling again. You're breathing softly next to me, finally at peace. I wonder what you dream about. I wonder if I'm there.

Fuck, this hurts.

I reached for you today. Not just with my hands. With everything. And I watched you flinch away like my love was acid. Like I was burning you with my need to be close.

I'm so tired of being your monster.

You called me controlling yesterday when I asked how your day was. Manipulative when I made dinner. Suffocating when I said I missed you. Every genuine feeling I have gets twisted into something ugly once it passes between us. My love becomes your proof that something must be wrong with me. Who could possibly love you, right?

I see how you look in the mirror. The disgust. The judgment. The ruthless cataloging of every flaw. I've memorized the exact sound of your sigh when you decide, yet again, that you're not enough. Not thin enough. Not smart enough. Not successful enough. Just... not enough.

And then you look at me with that same disgust. Because what kind of fool would choose you? There must be something wrong with me. There must be an angle. There must be a lie.

My friends think I'm an idiot.

They've stopped asking about you.

"Just leave," they say, like it's simple. Like you're just some bad habit I need to break. They don't understand that I've seen beneath the mask. I've caught glimpses of who you are when you forget to hate yourself for five fucking minutes. Those rare, beautiful moments when you actually let yourself believe you might be worthy of love.

God, those moments.

I live for them.

Store them up like a squirrel preparing for winter.

I know what this is doing to me. The weight I've lost. The friends who've drifted away. The constant knot in my stomach waiting for the next accusation, the next fight, the next door slammed in my face. I know I'm disappearing too.

Sometimes I think about leaving. Actually, that's a lie. I think about it every day. Every single day. I rehearse the speech. Pack imaginary bags. Close the door behind me and drive until the pain in my chest subsides.

But then night comes. And you're there, vulnerable in sleep in a way you never allow yourself while conscious. And I think maybe tomorrow will be different. Maybe tomorrow you'll believe me when I say you're enough. You've always been enough.

I don't know how much longer I can do this. Love shouldn't feel like drowning. But walking away feels like giving up on the one thing I still believe in – that underneath all this pain is someone fighting to believe they deserve to be loved.

And so I stay. I stay and I bleed and I hope.

Pathetic? Probably.

Real? Absolutely.

I’m too old for fairy tale. This is love with teeth and claws and endless, sleepless nights. This is choosing someone who can't choose themselves. This is believing in someone who looks at their reflection and sees only failure.

I don't know how our story ends. But I know that at 4:17 AM, watching you sleep, I still choose you. For better or worse. Mostly worse, these days.

And tomorrow, I'll try again. And you'll push me away.

And we'll both pretend not to be breaking into smaller pieces each time.
